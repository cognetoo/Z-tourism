Traditional Foods of New Zealand

Discover the unique flavors and culinary history of New Zealand

Hangi

Region: Nationwide, especially among the Maori
Taste: Earthy and smoky flavors with tender meat and vegetables
Origin: The Hangi is a traditional Maori cooking method using heated stones in a pit oven. Meat and root vegetables are wrapped and buried to slow-cook for hours, giving a unique flavor to the meal.

Pavlova

Region: Nationwide
Taste: Light, airy, and sweet with a crispy exterior and marshmallow-like interior
Origin: Named after the Russian ballerina Anna Pavlova, this dessert is believed to have been created in New Zealand. Topped with fresh fruits, it is a national favorite, especially during celebrations.

Kumara (Sweet Potato)

Region: Particularly popular in North Island
Taste: Sweet and starchy, with a soft texture when cooked
Origin: Originally brought by Maori settlers, kumara is a staple food with various varieties and is often included in Hangi meals or served roasted.

Kiwi Burger

Region: Nationwide
Taste: Savory, with a unique combination of beef patty, fried egg, beetroot, and more
Origin: The Kiwi Burger, with its unique toppings like fried egg and beetroot, is a quirky and iconic New Zealand take on the traditional hamburger.
